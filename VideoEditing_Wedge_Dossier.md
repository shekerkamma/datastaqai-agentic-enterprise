## 3. Emotional Friction — Verbatim & Near-Verbatim
- **The "30-second video" lie.** Somanath: *"The video was 30 seconds long. I'd been working on it for four hours… The '30-second video' is a lie. It's a 3–4 hour production job in a trench coat."* On who actually wins: *"Top creators have editors. Agencies have teams. Normal people have a day job and tired eyes."* (Source 1)
- **The Timeline Black Hole.** Creative AI Tracker: *"You have to scrub through massive travel vlogs just to find those snappy, 15-second hooks… It is incredibly soul-crushing work. You're scrubbing frame-by-frame, endlessly splitting clips, manually fixing audio levels, and typing out captions word-for-word."* And: *"If you are trying to meet this demand by manually editing every single piece of micro-content… You are an underpaid employee working for the algorithm."* (Source 2)
- **Rough-cutting is the breaking point.** Nichestarter (Reddit synthesis): *"Creators often find the rough cutting phase of video editing to be the most tedious and frustrating part of their workflow… time-consuming and mentally draining… contributing to burnout."* (Source 3)
- **The mechanical tasks, quantified.** Cutsio: silence removal on a one-hour interview = *"fifty to one hundred individual cuts just to remove dead air"*; social reframing = *"ten to fifteen minutes of hands-on work"* per 30-sec clip; these four tasks *"consume 60 to 80 percent of pre-editing time."* (Source 4)

## 4. User Psychographics
- **Roles:** Solo creators, social-video editors, agency post-production, content teams shipping daily short-form.
- **Emotional state:** Burned out, "eyes burning," trapped on the algorithm's treadmill, resentful that the format favors people who already had time/teams.
- **The acute bottleneck:** Mechanical post-production — clipping, silence/filler removal, captions, reframing for 9:16/1:1/16:9 — which is 60–80% of the time and zero of the creativity.

## 5. The Agentic Wedge
**Wedge name:** "First-Pass Editor Agent (Clip → Caption → Reframe)"

**How it works:** An agent that ingests raw footage and produces the mechanical layer automatically — highlight detection, silence/filler trim, word-synced captions, multi-aspect reframes, batch exports — then hands a staged timeline to the human for the emotional/creative last mile (no auto-publish).

**Why it wins:** It removes the proven 60–80% mechanical load (the "Timeline Black Hole") without touching creative judgment — exactly the RENEGOTIATE split. The NLE stays; the standalone reframing/caption point-tools and the volume grind compress.

**Buyer trigger:** A creator realizing "4 hours for 30 seconds" is unsustainable at daily cadence.

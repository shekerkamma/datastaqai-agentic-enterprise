## 3. Emotional Friction — Verbatim & Near-Verbatim
- **The treadmill, named.** Roster: *"Every DTC marketer knows the grind: launch, spike, tank, repeat. Your best creative burns out in two weeks, the algorithm demands more, and your team is already running on caffeine and chaos."* And: *"the harder you push, the less it performs."* (Source 1)
- **Iteration speed is the #1 blocker.** Starti, citing Statista 2025: *"over 63% of advertisers cited creative iteration speed as their biggest scaling blocker, followed closely by team burnout… Manually producing 100 ad versions to keep campaigns fresh is not sustainable."* (Source 2)
- **The hamster wheel turns marketers into assembly-line workers.** ContentDrive: *"This is content burnout, and it turns creative marketers into assembly-line workers."* Marketers spend *"often 80% or more"* of time on tactical execution — *"writing copy, resizing images, scheduling posts, and manually setting up dozens of ad variations."* (Source 3)
- **The manual reality, hour by hour.** Marketing Career Insights: *"Crafting social posts, emails, and graphics from scratch every cycle turns exciting ideation into a grueling chore… A single email campaign… might span 4-6 hours of tweaks… Teams end up working weekends to catch up."* (Source 4)

## 4. User Psychographics
- **Roles:** DTC/e-commerce marketers, content teams, campaign managers, marketing ops.
- **Emotional state:** Exhausted and numb — "running on caffeine and chaos," creativity drained by paraphrasing the same product benefits, working weekends to feed the machine.
- **The acute bottleneck:** Hand-producing dozens-to-hundreds of personalized copy/asset variants per campaign on a linear "one input = one output" model that can't match platform demand.

## 5. The Agentic Wedge
**Wedge name:** "Brief-to-Variants Content Agent (Modular, On-Brand)"

**How it works:** An agent that takes one strategic brief + brand voice and generates the full personalized content matrix — copy blocks, channel variants, email sequences, ad copy — as modular, swappable components tuned to audience signals, with the marketer curating rather than typing.

**Why it wins:** It breaks the linear "double the content = double the effort" model that powers the content treadmill, attacking the #1 cited scaling blocker (iteration speed) and the burnout behind it. The martech/campaign platform stays as orchestration; the per-seat content-production tools are displaced. REPLACE at the content layer.

**Buyer trigger:** Creative fatigue + a team "working weekends to catch up."

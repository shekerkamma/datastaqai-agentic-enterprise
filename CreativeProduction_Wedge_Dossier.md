## 3. Emotional Friction — Verbatim & Near-Verbatim
- **Resizing eats a quarter of the week.** Viewst: *"resizing alone can take up to 26% of a designer's workweek, with some campaigns requiring 40+ hours of adjustments… Many designers are expected to produce 5-10 designs per day."* And the emotional core: *"One experienced designer described the shift from feeling energized by creativity to feeling drained by it."* (Source 1)
- **The volume-without-variance trap.** Jason McCormick, LinkedIn: *"Why is our creative team burned out when we're only launching 20 ads per week? Because 18 of those ads get zero spend… When 90% of your team's output gets rejected by the algorithm before customers even see it, you're not testing effectively. You're wasting creative hours."* (Source 3)
- **People quit over it.** Hawky's case team: *"Within three months: two designers handed in notice citing 'no time to do actual creative work.' The third was spending 70% of their time resizing existing assets for different platforms."* (Source 2)
- **The platform out-paces the team.** Meta wants 3–5 fresh variants/week/ad set; TikTok creatives die in *"as little as five days"*; PMax wants 15 headlines + 5 images. *"Most creative teams were never built for this pace."* (Source 2)
- **It's an automation problem, not a headcount problem.** Alvin Ding: *"Most agencies discover that 60-70% of time goes to repetitive tasks (resizing, minor copy changes, formatting) rather than core creative strategy."* (Source 4)

## 4. User Psychographics
- **Roles:** Performance-marketing designers, agency creative-production teams, freelance ad-variant producers.
- **Emotional state:** Drained, demoralized ("feeling like an assembly line"), quitting — passion-hires reduced to mechanical resizing while the algorithm rejects 90% of the output anyway.
- **The acute bottleneck:** Generating dozens-to-hundreds of on-brand resizes/variants per concept, where 60–70% of the hours are pure mechanical reformatting.

## 5. The Agentic Wedge
**Wedge name:** "Variant Factory Agent (Brand-Locked, Strategy-Fed)"

**How it works:** An agent that takes one approved concept + locked brand rules and generates the full matrix of platform-sized, audience-tuned variants — handling the 60–70% mechanical reformatting load — while routing the human to the 30% that's actual creative judgment (hooks, surprising angles).

**Why it wins:** It collapses the proven resizing tax (26–70% of designer time) that no per-seat tool solved, and reframes the work from "volume grind" to "strategy + variance." Brand/DAM systems stay; per-editor production seats and the freelance long tail compress. REPLACE.

**Buyer trigger:** A creative team handing in notice — "no time to do actual creative work."

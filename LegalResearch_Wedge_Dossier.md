## 3. Emotional Friction — Verbatim & Near-Verbatim
- **The quote that started a "ruckus."** Solo lawyer Carolyn Elefant's LinkedIn post: *"It does not bode well that my $20/month ChatGPT subscription can give me a correct Bluebook citation for a public utilities commission order when my $270/month LexisNexis subscription cannot."* (Source 1)
- **The research time is enormous and half-collectible.** TheLawGPT, citing the ABA: *"the average lawyer spends 17% of their workday on legal research — roughly 5 to 10 hours per week. For associates and junior attorneys at small firms, that number reaches approximately 15 hours per week."* And: *"lawyers collect on approximately 70–75% of their billed research time — meaning 25–30% of research hours either go uncompensated or are absorbed by the firm."* (Source 2)
- **Duopoly contract-rage.** Vaquill: *"Cancellation friction. Getting out is deliberately harder than getting in. Lawyers have described cancellation processes that require a mailed physical letter inside a tight window."* Plus *"per-document surprises… sometimes over a hundred dollars for a single document."* For a three-person shop with AI: *"well over $1,000 a month on research before billing a single hour."* (Source 3)
- **Opaque, jumpy pricing.** Spellbook: *"Westlaw's pricing is known for being complex and is often custom-negotiated… Upgrading between tiers can result in significant cost increases, with some reports indicating jumps of 30% or more."* CLM gap: redlining *"require[s] a separate Word plug-in, which can create a disjointed experience."* (Source 4)

## 4. User Psychographics
- **Roles:** Solo and small-firm attorneys, associates, paralegals, in-house counsel.
- **Emotional state:** Indignant — paying AmLaw-economics prices for output a $20 consumer tool now matches, trapped by auto-renewal + mailed-letter cancellation, blindsided by per-document charges.
- **The acute bottleneck:** 5–15 hrs/week of lookup-and-draft, 25–30% of it unbillable, on a tool whose cost structure "does not flex for you."

## 5. The Agentic Wedge
**Wedge name:** "Lookup-and-Draft Agent (Citator-Verified)"

**How it works:** An agent that runs the case-law survey, statutory research, issue-spotting, and first-draft brief/redline loop, with citations grounded to official reporters (and a verification pass against KeyCite/Shepard's for the citator gap). Transparent seat pricing, no per-document traps.

**Why it wins:** It collapses the proven 5–15 hr/week research-and-draft tax — including the 25–30% that's unbillable — at a fraction of a $400+ AI-laden seat. The proprietary case-law corpus and citator stay valuable (hybrid), but the per-seat research licenses compress. REPLACE at the seat layer.

**Buyer trigger:** Renewal at a 30%+ tier jump, or the "$20 beats $270" realization going around the firm.

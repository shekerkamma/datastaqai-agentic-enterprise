## 3. Emotional Friction — Verbatim & Near-Verbatim
- **The verbatim forum complaint.** Bynder Community, user Glenn Bassett: *"We are having an issue when very similar files are showing up in our duplicate finder… If we want to keep both I guess they will forever show up on our duplicate finder list. Which after a while makes it time consuming to manage."* (Source 1)
- **The real problem is trust, not storage.** Stockpress (Reddit-sourced): *"People don't know which file is the approved version, so they either waste time asking someone or use whatever they find first. Old logos get used in proposals. Outdated campaign images appear in social posts."* The recurring question: *"Why can nobody find anything?"* (Source 2)
- **The 30-second adoption cliff.** VIM Group: *"If people can't find the right asset in under 30 seconds, they'll stop using the DAM."* Adoption falters because *"assets pile up without proper tagging, making search frustrating"* and teams *"revert to old processes."* (Source 3)
- **The hidden admin-labor line item.** Uplifted: neither Bynder nor Brandfolder's pricing *"includes the labor cost of a DAM admin who tags, re-tags, and polices metadata manually — the hidden line item both vendors bury."* And lock-in: *"The custom taxonomy you spent six months building does not travel."* (Source 4)

## 4. User Psychographics
- **Roles:** Creative-ops managers, brand managers, DAM admins, designers searching for approved assets.
- **Emotional state:** Frustrated and distrustful — can't tell which version is approved, tired of being the human "metadata police," quietly reverting to Google Drive.
- **The acute bottleneck:** Search depends on sustainable manual tagging that never happens; assets pile up untagged/duplicated and the DAM loses trust.

## 5. The Agentic Wedge
**Wedge name:** "Always-On Curation & Tagging Agent (DAM-Native)"

**How it works:** An agent that continuously auto-tags incoming assets (visual + contextual), detects true duplicates vs. intentional variants, flags/retires outdated versions, and answers "what do I need?" in natural language over the DAM — eliminating the manual metadata-policing role.

**Why it wins:** It removes the proven adoption-killer (untrustworthy, untagged libraries) and the buried DAM-admin labor cost — but it *feeds* the DAM rather than replacing it. The asset system of record stays and compounds in value as the agent fills it. KEEP — the agent enriches the moat; tooling spend shifts rather than disappears.

**Buyer trigger:** A DAM ROI review showing low adoption + the realization that "search quality depends on manual tagging without a sustainable governance model."
